# Commercial Strategy

WonderShow should use an open-core strategy rather than publishing the full commercial app.

## Recommended Split

Open source:

- project file schemas;
- sidecar and plugin contracts;
- sample projects and validation utilities;
- documentation for integrations.

Paid product:

- signed macOS app;
- stable capture and live monitor preview;
- complete export pipeline;
- polished presenter controls;
- advanced effects that are production-ready;
- templates, support, and commercial distribution.

## Why This Works

Developers get a real surface to build on, audit, and improve. They can write plugins, sidecars, validators, importers, and exporters without reverse engineering private files.

Creators still have a reason to pay because the hard product value is the reliable app experience: capture, preview, export, performance, updates, and support.

## Packaging Suggestion

Use three tiers when pricing is introduced:

- Free: WonderShow Core, docs, examples, community plugins.
- Pro: official signed app, stable recording, export, updates, and creator-focused workflow.
- Studio or Team: priority support, team workflows, templates, batch/export automation, and commercial deployment help.

The value metric should follow creator output and professional reliability, not source-code access.

## Community Promise

Do not present this package as the full app. Be explicit that WonderShow Core is the ecosystem contract and that the paid app is the complete creator tool.
